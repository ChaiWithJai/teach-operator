# Product Direction

## Who (JTBD)

People **suspected or late-diagnosed with ADHD**, plus **founders and high-agency builders**
whose brains work better with external structure.

> **Job:** "When my work is scattered and I can't start, help me turn my goal into a calendar
> I trust and actually do the work — without shame — using AI that does the boring parts."

They don't want another app to configure. They want their *existing* surfaces (calendar,
email, docs) organized by something that understands ADHD, and they want it to feel good.

## The wedge

**Deliver the source material AS the product.** The curriculum (Ali's "4 Steps to Hyperfocus":
Subtract → Add → Multiply) is not a video library — it's an **interactive game** that, while it
teaches, configures the user's real system through Codex:

- Onboarding = playing the 9 modules: each is one decision + one apprentice action.
- The payoff is real: a **calendar with the goal event, deep-work blocks, and protected
  routines** — created drafts-first, approved in one tap.
- Continued assistance = the same console becomes a daily/weekly game (streaks, repair loop).

The dopamine the course preaches is built into the UI: gold stars, focus points, confetti, a
report card. Completion is celebrated, misses are diagnosed (never shamed).

## Why it goes viral / dominates the community

- **Time-to-magic < 10 minutes:** you finish onboarding with an organized calendar, not notes.
- **Shareable:** the report card / "Hyperfocus" badge is made to screenshot and post.
- **Private & local-first:** nothing leaves the machine — trust is the wedge for this audience.
- **It actually does the work:** the apprentice (Codex) reads calendar/email/Drive and drafts
  real artifacts. Advice tools don't convert; doing tools do.
- **Speaks the language:** built by and for ADHD founders; the copy and mechanics are native.
- **Compounds:** recurring friction becomes new skills; the system gets more useful over time.
- **Cross-harness:** ships in Codex, Claude Code, and opencode — wherever the builder lives.

## Non-negotiable principles (make it workable for ADHD brains)

1. **One decision at a time.** Never a wall of fields. Low working-memory load.
2. **Dopamine on every win.** Instant, visible reward for each cleared step.
3. **No shame, ever.** Missed = system diagnostic, not moral failure. Blame the system.
4. **Drafts first, reversible.** No external change without a one-tap approval.
5. **Local-first.** Files are the source of truth; no SaaS, no remote sync by default.
6. **Fast.** Sub-second interactions; works offline; keyboard + reduced-motion friendly.

## What "perfect v0.1" means

See [`first-release.md`](first-release.md) for the definition of done and the milestone.
In one line: **it literally does the ADHD job for a real person, extends to a second, runs in
Codex / Claude Code / opencode, and has at least one life-impacting automation running.**
